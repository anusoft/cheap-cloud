Localized Compute Economics: A Comprehensive Cost Analysis of CPU and RAM across Major Cloud Providers in ThailandThe sovereign cloud landscape in Thailand has undergone a paradigm shift driven by billions of dollars in infrastructure investments from global hyperscalers. For organizations operating within highly regulated sectors such as financial services, healthcare, and government, transitioning from remote regional hubs like Singapore to localized data centers within Thailand's borders addresses two critical operational challenges: latency and legal compliance. Hosting workloads in localized Bangkok data centers drops round-trip latency from standard regional times of 30–50 milliseconds to a highly responsive 5–10 milliseconds, while facilitating absolute compliance with Thailand’s Personal Data Protection Act (PDPA).This comprehensive report delivers an exhaustive comparative unit-cost analysis of localized compute resources—specifically virtual Central Processing Units (vCPUs) and Random Access Memory (RAM)—across the major cloud providers with active or announced regions in Thailand: Amazon Web Services (AWS), Google Cloud Platform (GCP), Tencent Cloud, Huawei Cloud, Alibaba Cloud, and Microsoft Azure.Localized Data Center Footprint in Bangkok and Eastern Economic CorridorThe geographical distribution of cloud infrastructure in Thailand represents a mixture of live, fully operational regions and major developments that are currently under construction.
